What is foreign exchange?

Forex market is the world’s largest financial market. It penetrates all levels of society – from the most fundamental personal foreign currency exchange to corporations and national governments.

Investors buy and sell national currencies in the forex market, profiting from the price difference generated by the exchange rate. While buying Currency A, investors are also selling Currency B. Therefore, each transaction involves two types of currencies, forming a currency pair that is being treated as a whole. Major currency pairs are EUR/USD, GBP/USD, USD/JPY and more. The first currency in the pair is called the base currency and the other is the quote currency. The price of the currency pair is based on the base currency’s value.

The first currency in the pair is called the base currency and the other is the quote currency. The price of the currency pair is based on the base currency’s value.

World’s largest and most liquid market

Daily trading volume of the forex market is over US$5.3 trillion, which is 12 times more than futures market and 27 times of equities; thus has huge potential to generate profits.

Low capital, high returns

Forex is a leveraged investment and trades by using margins. Hence, it requires low investment costs, provides higher flexibility and amplifies profits. On the flip side, users must pay attention to the amplification of losses and make wise decisions.

May profit in both bear and bull markets; two-sided trading

Market fluctuations do not affect the profitability forex trading. Users have the possibility to profit from both the rising and falling rates of the currencies, they may choose to buy or sell either way. If the trend is in line with their prediction, then they will make profits.

May trade at any time

In contrast to other financial markets, forex is open for trading 24 hours a day, 5 days a week, so users can respond swiftly to market fluctuations at any time.

Comprehensive legal control and regulation

As a mature and active market, forex has comprehensive legal supervision and regulation that provide legal protection for users’ rights and interests.

Fair and impartial, no banker manipulation

Due to the global nature of forex, it is a market where participants range from individuals to national governments, the possibility of manipulation is close to zero. Manipulating the forex market requires massive funds, which demands the intervention of national governments and central banks, manipulation is therefore highly unlikely.
